2. PERMITTED USE CASES
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The standard per-device license ($5 USD per device) permits use in:

  - Residential access control (homes, apartments, small buildings)
  - Small and medium-sized business (SMB) installations
  - Small office environments
  - Co-working spaces and boutique hospitality (under 50 access points)
  - Single ATMs or isolated banking terminals (non-networked)
  - Individual safes and storage units in residential or SMB contexts
  - Single-site CCTV and surveillance systems
  - Luxury automotive (individual vehicles)
  - Any installation under 50 access points that does not require
    safety certification (FIPS 140-2, PCI-DSS, or equivalent)

The following use cases require a separate Enterprise License Agreement:

  - ATM networks, banking infrastructure, or payment terminal chains
  - Bank vaults, high-security safes, and critical asset storage
  - Installations of 50+ access points or 200+ devices
  - Multi-site chains (hospitality, retail, logistics)
  - Government, military, or critical national infrastructure
  - Any installation requiring FIPS 140-2, PCI-DSS, ISO 27001,
    or equivalent certification
  - Web-service infrastructure or SaaS platforms built on top of the SDK

The trigger for Enterprise licensing is scale + criticality + certification
requirements — not the industry sector per se. A single ATM in a small branch
may qualify for standard licensing; a national ATM network requires Enterprise.

4. DATA RESPONSIBILITY AND AUDIT TRAIL
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Software produces two distinct audit trails:

  (a) ON-CHAIN AUDIT TRAIL: NFT ownership events (mint, transfer, revocation)
      are recorded on Enjin Matrixchain and are public and immutable by nature
      of the blockchain. This covers ownership changes only — not physical
      access events.

  (b) LOCAL ACCESS LOG: Physical access events (who entered, when, with which
      role) are logged locally on the deployed device. This data does not
      transit through Licensor's servers. Licensor does not collect, process,
      or store any access log data.

Licensee is solely responsible for compliance with all applicable data
protection laws (including GDPR where applicable) with respect to the local
access log and any personal data collected or processed through the use of
the Software. Licensee shall implement appropriate data retention policies
and inform end users of any data collection in accordance with applicable law.
